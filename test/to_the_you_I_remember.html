A series of design artifacts that deconstruct the fleeting nature of human relationships by using shared memories from the past as a potential way to reconnect individuals in the present.

The Grey Area Between Stranger & Friend

To: The you I remember began as a fascination with the longevity and dissipation of human relationships — how we move from strangers, to friends or lovers, and then back to almost “strangers” again.

Despite technology giving us the advantage to connect and reconnect over boundaries that were much more prevalent before such as time and distance, the convenience of the medium often causes us to take these relationships for granted, with us comfortably knowing that they will continue to exist in this digital space.

To investigate and further my understanding of this phenomenon, I deconstructed 150 of my personal relationships through a printed data visualization piece, an interactive data piece, and a staggered concertina book — leading up to the final installation.

01 — Patterns of Relationships in a Physical Sphere

Are there patterns in relationships (A formula? An expiry date)? Is the longevity of a relationship dependent on the amount of physical interaction?

The first artifact deconstructs intimacy at a surface level by documenting the quantity of physical interactions I had with 150 individuals on a monthly basis within my 21 years. Each line represents a single individual and whether there was a physical meeting between the two of us. The data was printed on opalux in a continuous accordion scroll format.

02 — Expanding on Unique Relationships in a Physical-Digital Sphere

Are digital interactions an important factor in relationship patterns? What are each unique relationships built on and how do these "connectors" affect the strength of the relationship?

The second artifact deconstructs intimacy at a deeper second level by analyzing "connectors" which include mutual relationships, similarities in ethnic backgrounds, gender, age, geographic location, and interests. Through an interactive data visualization piece, users can explore moments of digital interaction and "connectors" that are/were prominent in my relationships.

03 — The Significance of Memory & Emotion

Can nostalgia and memory act as a device to develop and progress dissipated relationships?

The third artifact deconstructs intimacy by understanding the significance of memory and emotion as a tool to reflect on past and current relationships. On Thursday, November 10th, 2017 at 7:15am, I documented 20 of my most significant relationships in a staggered concertina book, writing down the most recent and prominent memory I have of the specific individual. A blind connecting contour in risograph accompanies each message, representing the last image and memory I have of them.

Concept — Brand & Event Application

The creation of the three artifacts led me to the idea of creating an installation and event that followed a temporal formula:

1. Relive the past.
2. Experience the present.
3. Leave a memento for the future.

The Installation & Event

In order for the project to resonate with each individual and propel their desire for reconnection, the event experience had to be personalized to each relationship and the memories that accompanied it. The installation had to have a singular base that could transition and change depending on the individuals interacting with it.

The installation is composed of a PVC frame with customizable layers of semi-transluscent fabric. Each layer represents a year shared within our relationship. During the event, projections are displayed from the farthest side of the viewer.

As the light from the projection passes through each layer, the memories projected fade and become blurry, representing the farthest memory which tends to be the least detailed. The viewer progresses through each layer when a year of memory is finished and as the viewer comes closer to me, the memories projected start to become clearer. The viewer can choose to stop progressing at any moment in the event. It is up to them whether they want to establish a connection or not.
